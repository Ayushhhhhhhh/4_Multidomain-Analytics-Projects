PROJECT 01

E-commerce Sales Analysis

An analysis of an Indian e-commerce store's sales data to identify key performance indicators, regional performance, and product profitability insights.

Key Insights Discovered:

Top Market Identified

Maharashtra leads all states in sales, marking it as the most critical market for growth and marketing focus.

Profit vs. Volume

Printers are the most profitable sub-category, while Clothing, despite making up 68% of sales volume, operates on lower margins.

Payment Preference

Cash on Delivery (COD) is the dominant payment method (44%), indicating a key customer behavior that impacts logistics.

Top Customer Base

Analysis reveals that Harivansh, Madhav, and Madan Mohan are the top 3 customers by sales amount.

Tools Used:

Power BI DAX Power Query

PROJECT 02

Music Store SQL Analysis

A deep dive into a music store's database using complex SQL queries to analyze customer value, genre popularity, and sales performance.

Key Insights Discovered:

Top Market for Promotion

Prague generated the highest total sales, making it the top candidate city for a promotional music festival.

Most Valuable Customer

Identified the single customer with the highest lifetime spending, providing a clear target for exclusive loyalty programs.

Dominant Music Genre

Rock music listeners are the most prevalent customer segment, suggesting marketing efforts should be focused there.

Top Performing Country

The USA has the highest number of invoices, confirming its status as the largest and most active market for the store.

Tools Used:

SQL PostgreSQL PgAdmin4

Key Analyses Performed:

  • Ranked countries by total number of invoices.
  • Identified the top-spending customer for targeted marketing.
  • Queried for top-selling genres to guide inventory.
  • Determined the most profitable city for a promotional event.
PROJECT 03

IBM HR Attrition Analysis

An analysis of IBM's fictional employee dataset to identify the primary drivers of attrition, culminating in an executive-level dashboard.

Key Insights Discovered:

Overall Attrition Rate

The company faces a significant **16.1% attrition rate**, with 237 out of 1470 employees leaving.

Salary is a Key Factor

A staggering **69% of employees who left** earned under $5,000 per month, pointing to compensation issues.

Departmental Hotspot

The **Research & Development** department has the highest number of departures (133 employees).

Early Career Turnover

Attrition is highest among employees with just **1 year of tenure**, indicating issues with early engagement.

Tools Used:

Power BI DAX HR Analytics
